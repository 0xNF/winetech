We strive to be honest so we will start with a confession:

When we were approached with a proposal to assist the owners of one of the country's finest wine cellars in putting it into our software, we jumped at the chance.

It is hard to say whether we were motivated more by the prospect of just seeing a cellar of over 25,000 bottles (including more than 4,500 larger format bottles) -- virtually all of them top rated by Robert Parker -- or by the challenge of seeing if our software was up to the task.

Believe us, it is massive:

This cellar was constructed under the owner's home by blasting it out of solid rock! It has grown over the years to accommodate a wine collection which knows few equals.

During the course of teaching the owners (who had very limited prior computer experience) we encountered a number of problems unique to a collection of this magnitude. Many of them had to do with managing the same wine located in multiple areas of the cellar and stored in different bottle sizes.

The result has pleased everyone. The cellar is now safely stored in a 300MHz Dell with 128MBs of RAM. We got invited to a fabulous wine tasting! Our clients benefited from enhancements we made and released in Version 3.5.

Curious to see this cellar?

The owner prefers to remain anonymous. This area stores some of the Italian wines, a particular favorite of the collector.

And here is a Visual Cellar Area from this part of the cellar (the colors represent Parker ratings):

The large rack shown here contains 960 bottles. Note that each bin contains two bottles which are shown one over the other - one of the options for representing "double deep" bin storage.

The Cellar Area is named to correspond to the owner's approach to naming physical locations within the cellar. The Live Bin Id is showing that we are pointing to bin "25-1" within this rack. The owner has chosen to represent each bin based on its row and column location. Thus this bin is in the 25th column over and the first row down.

The full cellar contains 42 Cellar Areas identical to this one and 19 other Cellar Areas for other types of storage, such as full cases!

You are probably wondering about performance. How fast can the software be when dealing with a cellar of this size? Well, in the words of the cellar's owners, "We find it a challenge to keep up with it!"
